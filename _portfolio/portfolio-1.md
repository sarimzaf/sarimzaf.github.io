## Overview

This project investigates whether unconditional cash transfers improve psychological wellbeing beyond their direct income effects. Using data from a large-scale social protection program in Pakistan, I examine outcomes including depression scores, perceived stress, and life satisfaction among beneficiary households.

## Research Questions

- Do cash transfers reduce psychological distress among recipients?
- Are mental health gains driven by income effects alone, or do transfers also reduce anxiety about future consumption?
- Do effects differ by gender of the household head or recipient?

## Data & Methods

The project uses household panel data collected at baseline and two follow-up waves. I employ difference-in-differences with geographic eligibility cutoffs as an instrument for program receipt.
