Overview

Despite gains in girls' enrollment over the past two decades, significant gender gaps in educational attainment and learning remain in rural South Asia. This project examines the household-level and community-level factors that explain these persistent disparities, with policy implications for targeted interventions.

Research Questions

  • What household characteristics predict differential enrollment by gender?
  • How do local labor market conditions affect families' returns to girls' schooling?
  • Which intervention designs are most effective at closing gender gaps in learning outcomes?

Data & Methods

The project draws on nationally representative household survey data and links it with school administrative records. Analysis combines regression discontinuity designs around school fee thresholds with qualitative interviews with parents and school administrators.
