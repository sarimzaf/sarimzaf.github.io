## Overview

A core question in development economics is why some households remain poor across generations despite access to credit and markets. This project investigates the role of behavioral factors — specifically risk preferences and aspirations — as potential mechanisms sustaining poverty traps.

## Research Questions

- Are risk preferences endogenous to poverty, or do pre-existing preferences predict poverty trajectories?
- Do aspirations set a ceiling on investment, and can low aspirations be shifted through informational interventions?
- How do these factors interact with access to social protection programs?

## Data & Methods

The project combines incentivized experiments to elicit risk preferences with longitudinal household survey data. A randomized aspiration intervention provides identifying variation for the aspirations channel.
